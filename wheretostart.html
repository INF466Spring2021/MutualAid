Identify the Issue

If you are considering starting your own Mutual Aid Society, chances are that you already have an issue in mind. When looking for additional members or volunteers for your group, it helps to have a clear, concise issue to address. Some questions to consider are what change you would like to see, what that change would look like, and what your visions are for the future of your Mutual Aid Society.

Sometimes an idea can grow beyond its original vision. In 1969, the Black Panthers started a free lunch program to prevent starvation in Los Angeles. By the following year, the program was feeding tens of thousands of children across several different cities. Checkout its history. 

Another thing to consider is whether a similar mutual aid organization already exists. If one does, it is worth asking what help they can use! See our map tool to determine if there is an already-existing Mutual Aid organization in the Capital District area.
